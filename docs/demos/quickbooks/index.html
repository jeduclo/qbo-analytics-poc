What This Dashboard Actually Does

Three capabilities that QuickBooks Online cannot provide on its own.

🎯

Collections Priority — Before Invoices Go Overdue

The Collections Priority table on the first dashboard page ranks every open invoice by its probability of going overdue — before it does. Each row tells you the customer, the balance, the probability score, and why that invoice is flagged: "Customer historically pays late," "Rising interest rate environment," or "No recent payment activity." The final column shows the daily financing cost of each day of delay at the current Bank of Canada rate. Your team opens this list every morning and knows exactly who to call and in what order — without a spreadsheet, without a manual aging review, without waiting for end-of-month reports.

📈

Three-Scenario Revenue Forecast

The Scenario Forecast page shows where revenue is headed over the next twelve months under three macro conditions: Base (current Bank of Canada rate unchanged), Stress (rate rises 75 basis points, inflation elevated), and Upside (rate cut begins, sector conditions improve). The forecast combines a time-series model trained on your payment history with a machine learning layer that incorporates live macro indicator data. The question it answers is not "what happened last quarter" — it is "which scenario am I planning my hiring and cash flow against right now." Switch between scenarios with a single click.

📋

Financial Statements From Your Chart of Accounts

The Financial Statements page generates a structured P&L and Balance Sheet directly from your QuickBooks Chart of Accounts — no manual export, no pivot table, no waiting for your accountant to run the report. Every account is mapped to its financial statement line, sign convention, and working capital classification. Current month versus prior year variance columns update automatically with conditional formatting: green when margins improve, red when they compress. Four financial ratios — Current Ratio, DSO, Gross Margin, and Collection Efficiency — sit above the statements as real-time reference points against industry benchmarks.

How It Works

Three layers, running unattended every morning.

1

Data

A Python script connects to the QuickBooks Online API each morning, extracts your customers, invoices, and payment history, and loads everything into an Azure SQL database. Bank of Canada rate and CPI data update alongside your financial records automatically.

2

Intelligence

Three machine learning models run against the updated data: an XGBoost classifier scores every open invoice for overdue risk; a Prophet model extended with a macro adjustment layer generates the twelve-month revenue forecast; an Isolation Forest flags statistical anomalies in recent transactions. Models retrain weekly on newly resolved invoices.

3

Delivery

Power BI refreshes from the updated database at 7:00 AM UTC. The dashboard — with its collections priority list, scenario forecast, and financial statements — reflects last night's data by the time your team starts their day. No manual steps, no exports, no waiting.
